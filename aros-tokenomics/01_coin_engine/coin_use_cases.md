# Coin Use Cases

ArosCoin (ARO) is engineered as a utility asset that enables and rewards productive economic
activity within the AST ecosystem. The token is not positioned for passive speculation; each use
case is backed by measurable services or risk management functions.

## Primary Utility Streams

1. **Transaction Fuel** – Fees for executing smart instructions across the Processing Layer are paid
in ARO. Fees are algorithmically adjusted according to network load and are recycled back into the
reward pool for validators and ecosystem grants.
2. **Collateral for Bridges** – Institutions bridging assets through the compliance gateway must post
ARO as collateral. This ensures that malicious behaviour is disincentivised and provides a reserve to
cover slashing events.
3. **Staking & Security** – Validators stake ARO to participate in consensus. Performance metrics and
uptime influence staking rewards, reinforcing the Proof of Transaction model.
4. **Governance Access** – Holding ARO enables participation in governance proposals proportional to
a compliance-adjusted weighting scheme.
5. **Ecosystem Services** – Service providers can denominate analytics, compliance attestation, or
liquidity provisioning in ARO, with the Bridge Layer offering automated conversions where necessary.

## Secondary Utility Streams

- **Treasury Grants**: Innovation grants and sustainability funds distribute ARO to projects that pass
the governance due diligence framework.
- **Insurance Pools**: Supervisory vaults accumulate ARO to underwrite operational incidents and
validator slashing penalties.
- **Reputation Metrics**: AI agents attribute behavioural reputation to addresses; high reputation
unlocks lower fees and priority bridge processing, creating demand for holding ARO as reputational
collateral.

## Compliance Considerations

All utility streams respect regulatory categorisation. KYC/AML protocols are enforced through the
Bridge Layer before an entity can leverage ARO utility. Transaction metadata is stored in a privacy-
preserving format so that auditors can verify legitimate use without revealing confidential details to
the public ledger.

## Lifecycle

A user’s ARO position can move across custodial states: earned via rewards, deployed in staking, used
for fees, or returned to the treasury via burn operations. The Coin Engine tracks this lifecycle,
ensuring supply, demand, and compliance obligations remain in balance.
