# Global Threat Model

Consolidated view of risks impacting the AST ecosystem and mitigation strategies.

## Threat Categories
- **Economic Manipulation**: Exploiting emission rules, liquidity pools, or staking incentives.
- **Validator Compromise**: Unauthorized access to validator nodes, key leakage, or collusion attacks
against PoT consensus.
- **Bridge Exploits**: Fraudulent KYC bypass, cross-chain replay, oracle tampering, or custodial breach.
- **AI Subversion**: Poisoning of training data, adversarial inputs, or downtime of supervisory agents.
- **Governance Abuse**: Rogue proposals, quorum manipulation, or denial-of-service on escalation paths.
- **Regulatory Non-Compliance**: Failure to meet reporting, travel rule, or data residency obligations.

## Mitigations
- Multi-factor validator authentication, HSM-backed signing, and behaviour scoring monitored by AI
agents.
- Real-time liquidity monitoring with automated circuit breakers, reserve buffers, and emergency
protocols.
- Decentralised transaction encoding and audit trails enabling forensic reconstruction.
- Segregated AI training pipelines, bias testing, and human oversight for high-impact decisions.
- Governance guardrails enforcing proposal vetting, stake-weighted verification, emergency rollbacks,
and supervisory veto rights.
- Compliance automation with periodic audits and regulator engagement plans.

## Residual Risk Management
- Maintain cyber insurance and capital buffers for extreme scenarios.
- Run regular tabletop exercises with validators, compliance teams, and regulators.
- Update threat model quarterly based on incident learnings and emerging intelligence.
