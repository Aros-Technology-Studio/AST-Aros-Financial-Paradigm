# **🔐Legal Commentary on Asset Backing & Double Issuance Prevention**

### **1.Asset-Backed Token Model**

ArosCoin is issued exclusively as a response to verifiable incoming assets, either fiat or cryptocurrency. Every token minted corresponds **1:1** to an actual value **already received and settled** through ALB (Aros Liquidity Banking) or AST (Aros Studio Tokenomics), and is **immediately recorded in the on-chain reserve ledger.**

### **2.Immutable Conversion Registry & Emission Lock**

All token emissions are registered within the **Conversion & Custody Registry**, a core component of the AFC protocol, and enforced by the **Immutable Execution Contracts** of the AST layer. This registry ensures that:

- Each asset is tokenized **once and only once**,
- Every token corresponds to a **verifiable on-chain record** of fiat or crypto input,
- No secondary issuance can occur without **burning** the original token first (rollback or redemption mechanisms).

### **Reserve Transparency & Independent Verification**

All reserves (crypto or fiat) are stored in **segregated custodial pools** within AFC-controlled environments. The system provides:

- Real-time reserve auditability via external oracles and internal APIs,
- Full traceability of each transaction from source to token issuance,
- Public access to non-sensitive conversion logs and circulating supply.

### **4.Legal Liability Distribution**

In case of fraud, misrepresentation, or emission error, the liability is strictly distributed:

- **ALB** is responsible for verifying fiat-originating transactions,
- **AST** is responsible for crypto-originating flows and token logic,
- **AFC (central governance)** ensures protocol compliance and security audits.

This distributed governance structure avoids **concentration of liability**, protects the users, and meets international compliance standards such as MiCA, FinCEN, and FATF.
